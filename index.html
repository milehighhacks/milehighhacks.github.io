Frequently Asked Questions

What is SummitHacks?

A hackathon is an event where a large number of teams (or individuals) come together to build something cool together for 24 – 48 hours (sleep is optional). Most projects made by teams usually take the form of mobile, web, or hardware application. SummitHacks is vacation style hackathon that takes place on a cruise ship! At SummitHacks, you will enjoy the variety of luxury type treatments and accommodations while you code, and visit another country for some brief entertainment.

What should I expect?

Hackers will all meet in Fort Lauderdale to debark from the port on Royal Caribbean’s Ocean of the Seas cruise ship, where they will have the luxury of rooms with complete bathroom amenities, unlimited supply of food, access to the pools, entertainment, and spas. They will hack for 7 days with stopovers to a private island (off the coast of Haiti), Jamaica and Mexico, where they’ll be able to explore each city’s citing. At the end of the hacking period, participants in their teams will demo their projects in front of judges, investors and recruiters.

Who can participate?

All college students, graduate students, and professionals are eligible to be a part of this exclusive journey! We have no age restrictions, and if you don’t fit into any of the categories above, but still want to be a part of it, contact us, and we’ll work something out!

How do I get to the debarking location?

All participants are expected to make their way to Fort Lauderdale (specific location to be announced) on their own. We will however have assigned buses coming from specified various locations all over the country (as well as Canada) to Fort Lauderdale. Unfortunately, we cannot provide travel reimbursements this time around.

When is it?

The expected launch date will be late May 2015 or early June 2015. Please stay tuned for further updates for a specific date.

What should I bring if accepted?

You would need your laptops, chargers, passports. If you have a certain hack in mind, please bring your hardware. Otherwise, you should be able to use the hardware we provide. Hardware available will be announced closer to the time of the event.

Can I start working on my project, or continue an existing one?

No. To make the hackathon experience authentic, participants should not be working on any projects beforehand. You are however allowed to install all third party APIs and libraries you may need for your hack. You can also start familiarizing yourself with the tools that you need for your project.

This all sounds too good to be true! Will it be expensive?

The general admission fee is $200, which covers all the accommodations of the entire trip.
